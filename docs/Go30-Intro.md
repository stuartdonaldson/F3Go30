# Go30 Intro & Explanation

Go30 is a monthly habit creation/destruction system for F3 Puget Sound PAX.

You are it! Both Warrior and Q of your own life. Headlock your trusted team and hold each other
accountable.

## The Core Mission

Execute a Daily Challenge that aligns with your Ultimate Goals, supported by the accountability of
your fellow HIMs.

## Three Layers of Change

Adapted from the Identity/Process/Outcome model in
[James Clear](https://jamesclear.com)'s *Atomic Habits* — see also his
[Habits Cheat Sheet](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1QX2KSlKxmgkaijfLK4QMrtniqydSkV1L/view).

Change happens in layers — outside in is the easy, usually-temporary way; inside out is what
sticks:

- **Identity (Who)** — WHO do you ultimately want to be? Change your beliefs about who you are.
- **Processes (How)** — HOW are you going to achieve this? Change your daily habits and systems.
- **Outcomes (What)** — WHAT would this ideal person do to be successful? Change your specific
  results.

Your repetitive actions — your habits — generate your results and express who you are. No single
action transforms your life, but consistency builds your identity.

Each PAX has an intimate, personal reason for his HC to Go30. Dig deep to find yours; it's what
carries you on the days motivation doesn't.

## Your Daily Mission

Each day scores one of three ways: **Hit** (`1`) if you did it, **Miss** (`0`) if you didn't, or
**Failed to report** (`−1`) if you forget to check in — so it always pays to report, even a miss.

1. **Choose your challenge** — small, specific, actionable (e.g. 15 minutes of stretching, daily
   journaling). It should be easy to score as a plain hit or miss.
2. **Sign up** — enter your F3 name and email on the Go30 signup page. If you signed up before, it
   recognizes you and offers to carry your goals forward.
3. **Record your score** — tap **I Hit it!** / **Missed it** each day on the Go30 check-in page
   (link in your confirmation email). The first time you identify, you land on a personal,
   bookmarkable check-in link — save it so future visits skip the form entirely. Right after you
   check in you'll see your dashboard: streak, total score, weekly bonus points, and your team's
   board. All Go30 data lives in a shared Google Sheet called the Tracker — you never need to open
   it, but if you prefer, you can check in by entering `1`/`0` directly in its Tracker tab instead
   of using the check-in page; either way updates the same record.
4. **Beat the deadline** — report by 10:00 AM the next day. There's a short grace period, but it
   expires that same day; after that the system automatically marks it a −1 for you, so don't rely
   on it.

## Strength in Numbers

You can go it alone — but you won't go as far. Team up with 3–5 fellow HIMs who share your drive,
usually grouped by AO since Shieldlock works best with proximity: hold each other to the standard,
call out the misses, celebrate the wins. Joining a team is optional but highly encouraged — you
can also participate solo.

Men who pursue hard things together become men who achieve them.

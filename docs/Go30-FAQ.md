# Go30 FAQ

## What is Go30?

Go30 is a monthly habit and goal tracker for F3 Puget Sound PAX. It helps men accelerate their
lives by executing a Daily Challenge, tracking progress, and staying accountable with fellow HIMs.

## What is the Daily Challenge?

A Daily Challenge is a small, specific goal aimed at building a positive habit or breaking a
negative one.

## What Daily Challenge should I choose?

Pick something actionable, well-defined, and challenging but sustainable.

Examples:

- No phone use while driving unless on speaker.
- 15 minutes of stretching daily.
- No eating after dinner or 8pm, whichever is earlier.
- Log all food and stay within 10% of calorie target.
- Journal daily for 15 minutes or 2 pages.

A good Daily Challenge should be easy to score as a plain Hit or Miss.

## How do I sign up for Go30?

Check Slack near the end of the month — a link to the Go30 signup page usually circulates about
5 days before the new month, and you can also sign up anytime during the month. Open the link,
enter your F3 name and email, and follow the steps. If you signed up before, it recognizes you
and offers to carry your goals forward — confirm and skip to the end, or make changes.

See the Go30 Info and Links canvas page linked at the top of the `#go30` channel.

## How do I record my daily scores?

Use the Go30 check-in page — the link is in your confirmation email, or on the Go30 Info and
Links canvas page. Enter your F3 Name and email once; after that, the app remembers you. Tap
**I Hit it!** / **Missed it** for today — if yesterday was left blank, it offers that too. Tapped
the wrong button? **No Check-in** clears it back to blank so you can redo it.

The first time you identify, you land on a personal check-in link — bookmark it (or Add to Home
Screen) so future visits skip the name/email form entirely.

After you check in, you'll land on your dashboard: current streak, best 30-day streak, a 7-day
rolling average, your month-progress ring, your team's tile, and the full PAX board.

You can also check in from the Go30 Tracker sheet — the shared Google Sheet where all Go30 data
lives. Enter `1` for a Hit / `0` for a Miss directly in the sheet, and it updates the same record.
From the check-in page, tap the chart icon (📊) any time to open that spreadsheet directly.

## I signed up for Go30. Now what?

Act on your Daily Challenge and report your score daily on the Go30 check-in page (or directly in
the Tracker sheet, see above).

## How is my daily score calculated?

Each day scores one of three ways: **Hit** (`1`) if you did your Daily Challenge, **Miss** (`0`)
if you didn't, or **No-report** (`-1`) if you forget to check in by the deadline — so it always
pays to report, even a miss.

## Why do I have a -1?

You did not report a Hit or Miss by 10am the following day.

A `-1` does not necessarily mean you failed your Daily Challenge — it means you missed the
accountability deadline. There is a short grace period after 10am, but it expires later that same
day; after that the system automatically applies the `-1` for you. Don't rely on the grace period —
report by 10am the next day.

## Can I reuse last month's goals?

Yes. Open the Go30 signup page and enter your F3 name and email. If you signed up last month, it
recognizes you and offers to carry your goals forward — confirm and move straight to the end, or
make changes.

## Can I change my signup registration?

Yes. Open the signup page again and enter your F3 name and email — it finds your existing
registration and lets you update it, including your email address, Team, and Nag selection.

## How can I get bonus points?

Bonus points are available for F3 engagement beyond your Daily Challenge.

- **Fellowship:** Participate in non-AO activities with Go30 PAX, such as an unpublished OTB
  workout with other PAX or coffeeteria. 1 point per week.
- **Q a Workout:** Lead a workout. 1 point per week.
- **EH an FNG:** Bring a Sad Clown into F3. 5 points per EH, no limit.
- **Inspire:** Post something motivational, progress-related, or encouraging in the Go30 channel.
  1 point per week.

## How do weekly bonuses work?

Bonuses are tracked Sunday through Saturday.

Occasionally, months align so you can earn extra bonuses. This is not a glitch — it's a small
reward for consistency and effort.

## Why are bonus points available?

Bonus points encourage fellowship, Qing, EHing, and engagement in the Go30 channel.

## What do bonus points have to do with habit change?

Your Daily Challenge is the main habit work. Bonus points support that work by encouraging
community engagement, leadership, and outreach — Go30 works best when PAX are connected and
encouraging each other.

## How do I record Bonus Points?

From the Go30 check-in page (or your dashboard), tap the trophy button (🏆) to list, add, or edit
your own bonus entries — no need to open the spreadsheet. **Q a Workout**, **Inspire**, and
**EH an FNG** require a Slack link (Backblast, Go30 post, or EH proof) before they count.

You can also use the legacy **Bonus Tracker** tab and enter the required details there instead —
both update the same tracker. Use the pulldown menu for fellowship points.

## Why aren't my Bonus Points showing?

Possible reasons:

- You did not log them (via the check-in page's bonus section or the Bonus Tracker sheet).
- You reached the weekly cap for that bonus type.
- You entered the wrong date, such as the wrong month or wrong year.
- A required Slack link is missing — EHing an FNG, Qing a workout, and Inspire all need one before
  they count.
- The Go30 sheet is under construction.

For help, contact the Go30 Q through the Go30 Info and Links canvas page linked at the top of the
Slack channel.

## Why are there teams?

Teams provide accountability and encourage fellowship. Shieldlock works best with proximity, so
teams are usually grouped by AO.

## How many men can be on a team?

Ideally, 3–5 men make a strong accountability team. More or fewer is fine if the team agrees.

## Can I participate remotely?

Yes.

## Do I have to join a team?

No, but it is highly encouraged.

## Can I participate solo?

Yes — choose **Solo** for your AO on signup.

## Why isn't my AO available on the Go30 signup?

Your AO may be new, underrepresented, or not have enough PAX signed up for a viable team. You can:

- Spread the word and recruit more PAX from your AO.
- Choose **Goal based - or other team** and enter a team name of your choosing.
- Choose **Solo**.

## What software will I need for Go30?

Just a phone or computer browser — the signup and check-in pages work on either. Having Google
Sheets on your phone makes it easier to check the Tracker sheet directly if you want to.

## What if I'm out of cell or Wi-Fi coverage?

Arrange with teammates to log scores for you, on the check-in page or directly in the Tracker
sheet. You can also log in advance and correct it afterward if needed. If you miss a day, change
the score to `0`.

Go30 is an honor system.
